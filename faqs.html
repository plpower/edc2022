How will ADUs affect my community?

While it is true that ADUs may lead to an increase in population density, as each ADU can take years to build, communities will not see an impact immediately. Nevertheless, many cities have room to accomodate more residents without decreasing the quality of life, as proven by Vancouver, whose ADU market has boomed since its legalization in 2009.

How big can an ADU be?

For lots between 5,000 and 7,500, ADUs can be up to 500 square feet. For lots between 7,500 and 10,000 square feet, ADUs can be up to 640 square feet. For lots greater than 10,000 square feet, ADUs can be up to 800 square feet.

Why have I never heard of ADU’s?

Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs) are also known by many other colloquial names such as carriage houses, granny flats, in-law units, or secondary suites, to name a few. The legalization of ADUs in America has only begun gaining traction within the past decade, Oregon and California as the trailblazers of the movement. While laws need time to be put into action, it shouldn’t come as a surprise if talk about ADUs becomes relevant in your municipality.

What’s the difference between an ADU and a guest house?

Generally, the main differences between a guest house and an ADU are that an ADU has a kitchen, while a guest house does not and that guest houses are typically smaller than ADUs. These definitions can vary by location though, so it is best to consult your local housing code for the best information.

Who lives in ADU’s?

In terms of household size, 57% of ADU resident households consist of one person and 23% consist of two people. Age wise, 22% of ADU residents in Portland, Oregon (a city with a significant sample size of ADU dwellers) are 55+ years old, while the median age of residents is 36.46% of those living in ADU’s are in an arms’-length relationship to the homeowner.

What is the Chicagoland housing crisis?

There are two extremes to the Chicagoland housing crisis. On one hand, there are areas that are lacking investment with decaying buildings, but on the other, there are areas that suffer from too much investment. Landlords are renovating the buildings, jacking up rents, and forcing tenants out of them. This increases the affordable housing gap and leaves many working-class tenants struggling despite the developments in the economy.

How will ADU’s help with the housing crisis?

ADUs increase housing affordability as not only do they increase the supply of housing units in general, but the rent for ADUs are also usually the third of the rates of other new housing units. We don’t have a lot of space to build new buildings, so the ADUs allow for the smaller unused areas to be utilized.

Will ADUs in my community affect parking?

While ADUs can mean a slightly increase in the number of cars in your neighborhood, the concerns around parking, traffic, and congestion are generally thought to be bigger issues than they are in reality. ADU dwellers own less than one car per household (while the average American household has around 2), and cars belonging to ADU households make up a small sliver of the total cars in a town or city.
