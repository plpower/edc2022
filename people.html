What is an ADU?

Accessory Housing

Accessory Dwelling Units come in all shapes and for many purposes. They serve as an additional unit to primary housing.

Small in Size

ADUs are typically much smaller than an average US house as they are built often as an accessory to a larger home.

Ownership

ADUs are typically owned by the owner of the adjacent primary housing; the ADU can be rented or set aside for family members.

ADU Legality

ADUs are largely illegaal accross municipalities in America, but as of recent there is a movement to legalize them as a way to combat housing crises.

Development & Building

ADUs tend to be built separately from their adjacent primary housing.

Aging at Home

Many older ADU residents are attracted to the accessory housing because it offers a way for them to grow older in a local setting.

Small house for smaller household

As family sizes keep decreasing, ADUs make more sense for smaller families who require less physical space.

Affordability

ADUs provide the option of affordable housing in communities that would otherwise be inaccessible to lower-income families.
