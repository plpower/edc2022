What is an ADU?

Accessory Housing

Accessory Dwelling Units can take many forms, ranging from basement conversions to garage redesigns. A few of the most common forms of ADUs are as follows:
Detached: A stand-alone home, typically converted from a backyard bungalow or other outbuilding.
Attached: Connected to the main home through the construction of an addition.
Garage Conversion: An addition to or a simple conversion of a garage into a liveable space.
Internal Renovation: Basement conversion and upper level conversion are both examples of these.

Ownership

Most municipalities that permit ADU construction are open to renting ADU’s to outside tenants. If an owner is building the ADU for their own use, such as in the common “retire in place” motive, ADU legislation allows that, too!

Sustainability

Given that the average ADU is 600-1,000 square feet, the energy required to heat, cool, and care for such a home is significantly lower than that of the average single family home. Fewer resources are required to construct these homes, and many of the construction firms at the forefront of the ADU movement utilize eco-friendly building materials and manufacturing processes.

Affordability

For both those who construct ADUs and those who live in them, ADUs are a dually affordable option. ADUs can generate rental income from tenants and there are no costs of purchasing land given that the land is already owned! From the renter’s perspective, ADUs are often the only affordable renting options in single-family neighborhoods, and many municipalities are subsidizing their construction for more affordable rental costs.

Lifestyle

ADUs are constructed as fully self-sufficient homes, with a living space, kitchen, bathroom(s), and bedroom(s). For retirees whose primary homes are too large for them, an ADU provides a perfect opportunity to “retire in place”. Young couples can take advantage of ADUs as a perfect starter home in neighborhoods typically above their price point, too.

Development

The ADU movement is real, and construction efforts are popping up nationwide. Contact us to find a local company, cooperative, or nonprofit looking to construct more ADUs in your area!
