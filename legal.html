ADUs in Chicagoland

ADUs were outlawed in Chicago in the 1950s due to concerns of overcrowded neighborhoods and decreased property value. This ordiance allowed exitsing ADUs to be acceptable but outlawed any new alternative dwellings on properties. Today, we are faced with a very different problem: property costs are prohibitively high which can displace residents from neighborhoods and discourage people from buying property in a certain community. ADUs are a way to address this housing crisis and can breathe new life into communities. Visit this website for more information on ADUs in the Chicago area.

Legalization Efforts in Illinois

Although ADUs are still not fully legal in Chicago, legalization efforts have begun across the state. At the state level, Rep. Robyn Gabel introduced a bill to make ADUs legal statewide (read the full bill here). Locally, some ogranizations have taken action to promote ADUs in their communities. In Evanston, IL, the Evanston Development Cooperative works to increase housing afforability, racial equity, sustainability, and community weath through the legalization of ADUS (check out their website here).

What can I do to help?

If you want to get involved in campaiging for ADU legalization contact your local/state representatives. You can also check out our Take Action page to message Evanston Development Cooperative about ADUs.

ADUs in California

California is one of the only states with state-wide ADU protections. In 2017, California passed ADU legislation that protected ADUs and made them easier to qualify for throughout the state. As of January 2020, California has further lowered the barriers to creating new ADUs. Below is some data on ADUs in California.
